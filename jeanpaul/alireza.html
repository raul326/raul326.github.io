Alireza Shojaian

Painter / Storyteller

About the Artist

Alireza Shojaian is an Iranian artist, born in 1988 in Tehran. He studied at IAU Art and Architecture University in Tehran, and obtained his masters in Fine Arts. He now spends time between Tehran and Beirut. Alireza Shojaian tries to showcase that which society tries to hide. He paints nostalgic faces, nude or lightly dressed bodies. The faces sometimes have a tense look, as if they were suffering. The spectator becomes part of the characters' intimacy, without much information. He or she is confronted to a state of suffering or embarrassment, but doesn't know why. The spectator is free to analyze as he pleases.

Insights into the Art

Insights into Alireza's Artwork

For more than twenty years I was hiding my identity, and now this identity is a subject for exhibition.
I'm trying to invite the people at least to listen; if they can't do anything, at least they can listen.
I was talking to the audience, I was sharing my personal life, the suffering that I always had to hide.
I have this hope that if it doesn't work for us, the next generation will live better.

by Alireza Shojaian
